1. **Overview**: - The MacBook brand replaced the PowerBook and iBook brands during the Mac transition to Intel processors in 2005. - The current lineup includes the **MacBook Air** (2008–present) and the MacBook Pro (2006–present). - Two different lines simply named "MacBook" existed from 2006 to 2012 and 2015 to 2019. - The MacBook family features unibody aluminum construction, backlit keyboards, and lids held closed by magnets. - Memory, drives, and batteries are now soldered or glued in place in the newer models. 2. MacBook (2006–2012): - As part of the Mac transition to Intel processors, Apple released a 13-inch laptop simply named "MacBook." - It served as an entry-level laptop, less expensive than the MacBook Pro and later the MacBook Air. - It was aimed at consumers and education markets and was the best-selling Mac during its existence. 3. MacBook Pro: - The MacBook Pro is the higher-end lineup in the MacBook family, introduced in January 2006. - It currently comes with 14-inch and 16-inch screens, using Apple silicon M-series chips.
